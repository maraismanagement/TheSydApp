Getting Around

Rideshare: Uber and Lyft are widely available in New Orleans.

Streetcar: The St. Charles streetcar line runs along St. Charles Avenue — a scenic and affordable way to get Uptown or to the French Quarter.

Biking: Blue Bikes are available at stations throughout the city.

Groceries

Nearby Grocery Stores +
  • Rouses Market - Full-service grocery store on Baronne Street, about 1 mile away.
  • Whole Foods - On Magazine Street in the Lower Garden District.
  • Dollar General - Quick basics and household essentials nearby.

Recommendations

Good For Groups +
  • Cochon - James Beard Award-winning Cajun cuisine. Great for large groups with family-style options.
  • Gianna - Italian-inspired restaurant on Magazine Street with shareable plates.
  • Mister Mao - Tropical roadhouse cuisine on Magazine Street. Fun, flavorful, and group-friendly.
  • St. Roch Market - Food hall with something for everybody.
Breakfast & Coffee +
  • Willa Jean - Upscale Southern brunch in the CBD. Try the famous biscuits.
  • Bearcat Cafe - Popular brunch spot; expect a wait but worth it.
  • District Donuts - Artisan donuts and great coffee on Magazine Street.
  • Cafe Du Monde - The iconic beignet experience. Open 24 hours.
A Nice Meal +
  • Commander's Palace - New Orleans' most famous restaurant. Jacket required.
  • Peche Seafood - James Beard Award-winning seafood in the Warehouse District.
  • Compere Lapin - Caribbean-meets-Creole fine dining at The Old No. 77 Hotel.
  • Herbsaint - French-Southern cuisine that's been a local favorite for years.
  • Shaya - Modern Israeli cuisine. Don't miss the pita bread.
  • Saba - Mediterranean restaurant on Magazine Street with excellent lamb and hummus.
Bars & Nightlife +
  • Bar Tonique - Craft cocktails in a relaxed setting.
  • Cure - James Beard Award-winning cocktail bar on Freret Street.
  • Frenchmen Street - The heart of live music. Walk the street and pick a spot.
  • Pat O'Brien's - Classic NOLA bar. Get a Hurricane and enjoy the courtyard.
  • Tchoup Yard - Large outdoor bar off Tchoupitoulas Street. Great for groups.
Entertainment +
  • National WWII Museum - One of the top museums in the world. Allow 3-4 hours.
  • Ogden Museum of Southern Art - Great art museum with Ogden After Hours on Thursdays.
  • Caesars Superdome - Home of the Saints. Check for events during your stay.
  • Smoothie King Center - Home of the Pelicans. Concerts and events year-round.
  • Jam NOLA - Immersive art experience celebrating New Orleans culture.

Neighborhood Itineraries

Uptown / Garden District +

Day

Brunch at Atchafalaya. Then stroll along Magazine Street for shopping and wander around the Garden District to check out the historic mansions. Stop at The Bulldog for a drink outside or for something fancier, head to The Columns Hotel.

Night

Head to Superior Seafood for one of the best happy hours in the city: $1.25 oysters and drink specials (4pm-6:30pm). You can either stay for dinner or continue on to Commander's Palace. Then head to Tchoup Yard or Miel, two large outdoor bars off Tchoupitoulas St.

CBD / Warehouse District +

Day

Start with breakfast at Willa Jean. Then walk to the WWII Museum or The Ogden Museum for engaging exhibits. Grab a mid-day latte at Mr. Wolf Espresso.

Night

For seafood dinner try Peche or Seaworthy. Next door is The Barnett for live music and drinks. Take the elevator to Alto, their rooftop poolside bar that closes at 10:30 PM.

French Quarter +

Classic FQ Night

Pre-dinner drink at Arnaud's French 75. Dinner at Galatoire's (jackets required) or Antoine's. After dinner, head to Pat O's for the courtyard and piano bar. Then walk to Caesars Casino for gambling.

The Bywater / Marigny +

Day

Brunch at Paladar 511 or The Country Club. Walk to Studio Be for art (opens at 2 PM, make a reservation). Walk to Parleaux Brewery, then make your way to Bacchanal for wine and live music.

Night

Dinner at Bywater American Bistro. Then walk to R Bar for a few drinks before making your way to Frenchmen Street for live music. The Spotted Cat, DBA, Blue Nile, and Maison are all good options.

Mid-City / City Park +

Day

Start with beignets at Cafe du Monde in City Park. Walk across the bayou to see the ancient live oak trees. Visit the New Orleans Museum of Art or Sculpture Garden. For lunch, Parkway Tavern has some of the best po-boys in the city.

Night

Pre-dinner drinks at Bayou Beer and Wine Garden. Then enjoy dinner at Addis NOLA for great Ethiopian fare. For music, The Broadside and Banks Street Bar have events nearly every day.
